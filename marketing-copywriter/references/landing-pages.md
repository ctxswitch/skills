# Landing Pages

Use this reference for landing pages, homepages, product pages, conversion pages, above-the-fold sections, CTA rewrites, and page critiques.

## Purpose

Landing pages carry one primary conversion. Their job is to make the right visitor understand the offer, believe the claim, and take the next step.

## Narrative Spine

Use this order unless the page context demands otherwise:

1. **Context**: who this is for and what they already believe.
2. **Problem**: cost of the status quo in time, risk, revenue, reputation, or effort.
3. **Outcome**: specific after-state.
4. **Mechanism**: how the offer delivers the outcome without drowning the reader in features.
5. **Proof**: evidence matched to the claims that need belief.
6. **Risk reversal**: trial, guarantee, migration support, security, compliance, onboarding, or transparent caveats.
7. **Single CTA**: one dominant action; use secondary actions only for hesitant but qualified visitors.

## Above the Fold

Hero block:

- **Headline**: promise, outcome, or category clarity. Avoid cleverness that hides what the product is.
- **Subhead**: clarify who it is for, how it works differently, or what risk it removes.
- **Primary CTA**: verb + object, such as `Book a 20-minute fit call`, `Compare plans`, or `Start a trial`.
- **Hero visual**: product, outcome, workflow, or social proof. Avoid generic imagery unless it supports positioning.

Microcopy matters. Form labels, button copy, reassurance text, and error states are conversion copy.

## Repeatable Body Blocks

- **How it works**: three steps max; tie each step to a user benefit.
- **Who it is for / not for**: filters wrong buyers and increases qualified leads.
- **Proof stack**: logos, metrics, testimonials, case snippets, third-party badges, screenshots, demos.
- **Objection handling**: price, switching cost, security, timeline, support, integration, training, compliance.
- **Final CTA**: repeat the primary action with consequence framing.

## Editing Pass

Ask:

- Would a buyer say this sentence aloud?
- Does each paragraph increase desire, trust, or clarity?
- Are hedge words doing work or hiding a weak claim?
- Is proof placed close to the claim it supports?
- Is the CTA specific enough that the user knows what happens next?
