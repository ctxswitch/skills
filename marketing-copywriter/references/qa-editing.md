# QA and Editing

Use this reference for polishing marketing copy, reviewing AI-assisted drafts, humanizing generic prose, checking claims, and preparing copy for publication.

## Core Rule

AI can accelerate drafts; it cannot replace judgment, facts, proof, positioning, or brand voice.

## Before Publishing

Check:

- names, dates, numbers, prices, product claims, and regulatory statements
- every claim that sounds sourced
- voice against the brand worksheet or known examples
- boilerplate phrases common in AI output
- stereotypes, exclusionary phrasing, or insensitive assumptions
- whether the CTA accurately describes what happens next
- whether the copy can be understood without internal context

## Where AI Helps

- brainstorm variants and angles
- rephrase for clarity after substance exists
- summarize long inputs the team has rights to use
- generate first-pass outlines
- pressure-test objections and alternate headlines

## Where Humans Must Lead

- positioning
- promises and guarantees
- sensitive claims
- customer quotes
- final client-facing judgment
- legal, financial, medical, security, compliance, or regulated copy

## Generic Copy Smells

Rewrite when you see:

- broad claims with no buyer, mechanism, or proof
- "In today's fast-paced world"
- "unlock," "leverage," "seamless," "robust," "game-changing" without evidence
- claims a competitor could paste their name into
- clever headlines that hide the category
- benefits that are just features with adjectives
- urgency without a real deadline
- three balanced bullets that say the same thing

## Rubric

Score each 0-2:

- **Factuality**: every concrete claim tied to evidence?
- **Specificity**: concrete nouns, numbers, examples, mechanisms?
- **Differentiation**: could a competitor use the same copy unchanged?
- **Reader fit**: does it address the right stage, trigger, and objection?
- **Voice**: does it sound like the brand rather than a generic helpful assistant?
- **Action**: is the next step clear, honest, and low-friction for the stage?
