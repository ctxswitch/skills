# Research and Proof

Use this reference before writing copy from scratch, reviewing vague copy, or building a brief. It distills research-first guidance for practical marketing copy.

## Core Rule

Copy built on assumptions becomes generic. Start with buyer triggers, objections, proof, competitor gaps, and search intent before drafting.

## Buyer Insight

Capture the buyer's job before choosing messaging:

- **Functional job**: what the buyer is trying to accomplish right now.
- **Social outcome**: how they want to look to peers, bosses, customers, or their team.
- **Emotional outcome**: what risk, frustration, embarrassment, confidence, relief, or urgency is present.
- **Category switch**: what they are replacing, such as a spreadsheet, agency, intern, incumbent tool, manual process, or doing nothing.

Persona one-pager:

```markdown
Segment:
Trigger:
Top objections:
Proof they trust:
Words they use:
Decision stage:
Desired next action:
```

## Competitor Lens

Compare competitors by:

- who they sell to
- core claim
- proof used
- onboarding or switching narrative
- pricing transparency
- guarantee or risk reversal
- repeated headline structures
- gap this brand can credibly own

Treat repeated competitor claims as category table stakes. Differentiation must be something the brand can prove, not a synonym for better.

## Website and Messaging Audit

For a homepage or key page, run a fast pass:

- Can a stranger say who it is for and what to do next within five seconds?
- Is there one obvious primary CTA above the fold?
- Are logos, metrics, quotes, demos, or badges near the claims that need belief?
- Are risk reducers visible where objections arise?
- Are there phrases a buyer would never say aloud?
- Does each key URL have a clear intent, missing input, and next experiment?

## Search Intent Inputs

Classify target queries by intent:

- **Know**: educate, explain, define, compare concepts.
- **Do**: complete a task or use a template/tool.
- **Buy**: evaluate vendors, pricing, alternatives, demos.
- **Go**: navigate to a known brand, product, or location.

Map each intent to one asset type: guide, landing page, comparison page, case study, product page, homepage, template, or demo page.

## Handoff Checklist

Before drafting long copy, confirm:

- buyer trigger is known
- top objections are named
- competitor gap is credible
- proof exists or is marked as missing
- page or email intent is clear
- CTA is tied to the reader's stage
