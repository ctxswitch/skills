# Case Studies

Use this reference for customer story interviews, case study outlines, proof narratives, testimonial editing, and customer-proof sections on pages.

## Before the Interview

Clarify:

- one clear outcome or metric the story will center on
- participants: practitioner, sponsor, buyer, implementer, or executive
- permissions for logo, quotes, metrics, screenshots, and exact wording
- legal/PR/compliance review if regulated
- competitor or sensitive data that must be redacted

## Interview Flow

For a 45-60 minute call:

1. **Context**: company, role, team, baseline situation.
2. **Status quo pain**: time lost, risk, revenue leakage, team friction, missed opportunity.
3. **Decision**: why they chose this solution over alternatives or inertia.
4. **Implementation**: what surprised them, what support mattered, what changed operationally.
5. **Results**: before/after numbers, timeframe, caveats.
6. **Forward look**: how they use it now, what comes next, whether they would recommend it.

## Probe Bank

Use sparingly:

- What would have happened if you had not changed?
- What almost stopped you from moving ahead?
- What did your team stop doing after implementation?
- What do you tell peers who ask about this?
- Which result mattered most internally?
- What caveat should a skeptical reader know?

## Story Spine

1. **Situation**: brief context.
2. **Complication**: what was going wrong.
3. **Resolution**: what changed and what the company did together with the solution/team.
4. **Evidence**: metrics, quotes, artifacts, examples, before/after.

## Quote Capture

Good quotes are short, specific, attributable, and sound like the customer. If a quote sounds like PR copy, ask for the same idea as if they were texting a colleague.

Avoid:

- overwritten corporate phrasing
- unsupported superlatives
- quotes that repeat the vendor's tagline
- metrics without owner validation

## Approval Checklist

Before publication:

- metrics validated with owner
- caveats noted
- quote sign-off complete
- logo usage confirmed
- sensitive data redacted
- legal disclaimer added if needed
- past-performance or regulated-industry claims reviewed
