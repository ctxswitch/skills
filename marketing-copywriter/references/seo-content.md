# SEO Content

Use this reference for SEO content briefs, search-driven articles, content outlines, on-page review, and SEO-focused marketing prose.

## Core Rule

A keyword without intent, angle, and success criteria produces generic copy. Build the brief before drafting.

## Brief Inputs

```markdown
Primary query/topic:
Intent type: Know / Compare / Buy / Local / Go
Reader profile:
Audience stage: awareness / consideration / decision
Jobs-to-be-done:
Unique point of view:
Primary CTA:
Success metric:
```

## SERP and Intent Notes

Look for:

- dominant formats: guide, list, product page, comparison, video, forum, tool, template
- featured snippet or AI overview opportunities
- questions the page must answer
- entities and concepts expected by the topic
- competitor gaps or weak angles
- whether the reader wants education, selection help, proof, or action

## Unique Point of View

Before drafting, answer: what can this brand credibly say that Wikipedia, a thin affiliate page, or a generic AI answer cannot?

Strong angles come from:

- first-party data
- customer experience
- operational method
- contrarian but evidenced point of view
- implementation detail
- templates, checklists, calculators, examples
- domain-specific caveats

## Outline Shape

```markdown
H1:
Intro job:
H2:
  H3:
  H3:
H2:
  H3:
FAQ or objections:
CTA:
```

Each heading should help the reader scan and decide whether the page answers their task.

## On-Page Essentials

- Title tag around 50-60 characters when practical, aligned with intent.
- Meta description as a compelling summary and CTA, not keyword stuffing.
- Internal links to and from related pillars, product pages, and proof assets.
- Schema only when justified by the content type, such as FAQ, how-to, article, product, or review.
- Images or diagrams only when they clarify the task, process, or comparison.

## Evidence and Compliance

Track:

- data points
- expert quotes
- customer examples
- source links
- compliance caveats
- claims needing SME review

## Measurement

Pick one leading success metric for the first review window:

- rankings
- clicks
- engaged sessions
- email signups
- trial starts
- demo requests
- assisted conversions
- internal link clicks

Avoid pretending a single article can own every business metric.
